# US Navy CUSA Portal — User Guide

This guide explains **how to use the website** step by step. It is written for people who may be new to the portal or to computers in general. If a section does not apply to your rank or job, you can skip it.

**Important:** Your organization’s **ranks, divisions, and policies** are configured in the system. What you see (menus, buttons, and data) depends on your account.

---

## Part A — Before you start

### What you need

1. **Internet access** and a **web browser** (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, etc.).
2. A **username** and **password** issued by your command (usually after an admin creates your account).
3. Optionally: **Discord** links or **screenshot files** if you will submit activity logs that require proof.

### Words you will see

- **Sign in / Sign out:** Logging into or leaving the portal.
- **Dashboard:** Your home page after login — summary of your activity and division.
- **Activity log:** Where you **submit** duty time or events for approval.
- **Admin panel:** Where authorized staff **manage people**, **approve submissions**, and configure divisions (you may not see this).
- **Pending:** Waiting for a reviewer to approve or reject.
- **Approved:** Accepted; it counts toward stats and (for events/duty) may count toward **quota** rules if your division uses them.
- **Division:** Your primary command group in the portal (for example a specific unit). **Navy Divisionless (NDVL)** means you are not assigned to a primary division in the system.
- **HQ / Headquarters:** A special division tier in the system; some features and quota rules work differently for HQ.
  - **Rank note:** In this portal’s rank list, the lowest HQ rank label is **MCPO**. Some HQ-wide admin features still require **CNP+** access in the software.

### The address (URL)

Open the portal using the link your command gave you. The **login page** is usually the site’s main page (`index.html`).

---

## Part B — Signing in for the first time

### Step 1: Open the login page

You should see:

- Title such as **US Navy CUSA** and **Personnel Management Portal**.
- Fields for **Username** and **Password**.
- A **Sign In** button.

### Step 2: Enter your username

- Click in the **Username** box.
- Type your username **exactly** as given (the system usually treats capitals/spaces in a forgiving way, but follow what your admin told you).
- Do **not** add `@navycusa.mil` or any email part — only the username.

### Step 3: Enter your password

- Click in the **Password** box.
- Type your password. Characters may show as dots — that is normal.

**Default password when an admin just created your account:**  
Often it is your **username** followed by **`1234`**. Example: if your username is `SailorSam`, try `SailorSam1234`. Confirm with your admin if unsure.

### Step 4: Click Sign In

- Click **Sign In** once and wait a few seconds.
- If login fails, read the **red message** under the form:
  - Wrong username/password: check caps lock and try again.
  - Too many attempts: wait a few minutes.
  - Account disabled: contact your chain of command.

### Step 5: First-time password change

If your account is marked for a **required password change**, you are sent to **Set New Password**:

1. You will see your username and instructions.
2. **New Password:** at least **8 characters**.
3. **Confirm New Password:** type the same password again.
4. Click **Update Password**.
5. After success, you will be redirected to the **Dashboard**.

If you see an error about **recent login**, sign out, sign in again, and immediately change the password.

## Part C — Layout of every main page (after login)

Most pages share the same chrome:

### Top header

- **Logo / NavyCUSA:** Often links back to the Dashboard.
- **Your username** and **rank label** (as stored in the system).
- **Avatar:** Usually the first letter of your username.
- **Sign Out:** Logs you out completely. Use this on shared computers.

### Left sidebar (navigation)

Links may include:

- **Dashboard** — home.
- **Activity Log** — submit duty or events.
- **QUOTA Management** — only for certain command ranks (see Part H).
- **Admin Panel** — only for authorized staff (see Part G).

On **phones and small screens**, there may be a **menu (☰)** button in the header to open the sidebar. Tap outside the menu or a link to close it.

### Main content area

The rest of the page shows tables, forms, and cards specific to that page.

## Part D — Dashboard (home)

Open **Dashboard** from the sidebar (or after login).

### Profile banner

Shows:

- Your **username**
- **Rank** badge
- **Category** badge (broad group such as HQ / Command / Enlisted — informational)
- **Division** badge

### Division activity (if you have a division)

You may see statistics for **your division**:

- **This week:** duty minutes and event counts (from **approved** logs that are still “live” in the system — not archived for weekly).
- **This month** and **All time:** include **archived** data as well, so numbers can be larger.

If you are **Headquarters staff (CNP+)**, you may see a **dropdown to pick a division** — choose a division to view its stats.

If you have **no division** or are **Navy Divisionless** in a way that excludes division stats, you may see a short message instead of numbers.

### Your personal counters

- **My Logs Approved:** how many of **your** submissions were approved.
- **Awaiting Review:** how many of **your** submissions are still **pending**.

### Recent submissions

A short table of your latest logs: type, details, date, status, submitted time.  
**View All** goes to the Activity Log page for the full list (on Activity page: “My Submissions”).

### Pending approvals (reviewers only)

If you are **MCPO or higher** (and your role allows review), you may see **Pending Approvals** — a quick list of others’ pending logs. **Review All** opens the Admin Panel’s review tab.

A **badge** on the sidebar next to Admin Panel may show how many pending items were found (when applicable).

### Quota status (if your division uses quotas)

If you belong to a **real division** (not NDVL), you may see **Quota status**:

- **Period** dates and **completion** summary.
- A **progress bar** (visual estimate).
- A **list of rules** and how you are doing on each (events vs minutes depends on the rule).
- **MDQRA** and **LOA** request forms (see Part H).

Badges may show **LOA exempt** or **MDQRA** reduction when applicable.

### Division reform list

If your command publishes a **reform snapshot**, you may see a table of members who are behind quota targets, plus a line stating whether **you** appear on that list.

## Part E — Activity Log (submitting duty and events)

Go to **Activity Log** in the sidebar.

### If you are HQ (CNP+) — division picker

You may see **Division for this log**. Use the dropdown to choose **which division** this submission belongs to. That division’s reviewers and event-type settings apply.

Everyone else submits for **their own division** automatically (no picker).

### Two tabs: Duty minutes vs Host event

- **Duty Minutes:** time you spent on duty.
- **Host Event:** an event you hosted (only shown if your division supports it — **not** for Navy Divisionless as primary division).

Click the tab you need. Only one submission flow is used at a time.

---

### E1 — Submitting a duty log

Stay on **Duty Minutes**.

1. **Duration (minutes):** enter a whole number (for example `60` for one hour). There are min/max limits (typically 1–1440).
2. **Date of Duty:** use the date picker for the day the duty occurred.
3. **Notes:** optional explanation.

**Proof (required — at least one):**

- **Upload Image:** choose a file from your device. Allowed types: JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP. Maximum size **5 MB**. A small preview may appear.
- **OR Discord Message Link:** paste a full URL to a Discord message (must look like a normal web link).

You must provide **either** an image **or** a Discord link (or both).

4. Click **Submit Duty Log**.
5. Wait for “uploading” and “submitting” states to finish.
6. Success: a green confirmation; the form may clear.
7. Your log appears as **pending** until a reviewer acts.

---

### E2 — Submitting a hosted event

Click **Host Event**.

1. **Event Type:** choose from the list. Types come from **global defaults** or your **division’s custom list** (set by admins).
2. If you choose **Custom Event**, a **Custom Event Name** field appears — you **must** type a name.
3. **Event Date:** date the event occurred.
4. **Participants:** optional count of attendees.
5. **Description:** optional text.

**Attendee usernames (quota credit):**

- Optional field: list **other portal usernames** who attended, separated by **commas** (or spaces).
- Each name must **exist in the portal** and be in the **same division** as this log.
- The **host** (you) is always credited automatically; you do not need to list yourself.
- If a name is wrong or wrong division, you will get an error listing the problem names — fix spelling or remove them.

**Proof:** same rules as duty — image and/or Discord link, at least one required.

6. Click **Submit Event Log** and wait for success.

**Note:** If you are on **Navy Divisionless**, you **cannot** submit hosted events through this flow; use **duty minutes** or ask HQ staff to submit on behalf of another division if your command allows.

---

### E3 — My Submissions table

Below the forms, **My Submissions** lists your logs:

- **Type:** duty vs event.
- **Details:** minutes or event name.
- **Date:** activity date.
- **Proof:** links to open image or Discord.
- **Status:** pending / approved / rejected (with reviewer notes if any).
- **Submitted:** when you sent it.

Use this to verify what was filed and whether it was approved.

## Part F — Change password (any time)

If you are **not** forced to change password, you typically change password through Firebase/your org’s process. The dedicated **change-password** page is mainly for the **first login** flow.

If you hit **Set New Password** again after already changing it, the site may redirect you to the Dashboard.

## Part G — Admin Panel (authorized users only)

The **Admin Panel** appears only if you have access: usually **CNP+** full admin, or **OCNP/OCNO** staff with sufficient rank for **Navy Divisionless** personnel only.

### Tabs you might see

1. **Personnel** — user list and add/edit.
2. **Log Review** — approve or reject pending logs.
3. **My division (events)** — MCPO+ in a single command division: edit **event types** only (simpler view).
4. **Divisions** — division list, webhooks, ranks, event types (depends on rank).
5. **Archive** — move approved logs into archives (high rank).
6. **Audit Log** — read-only history of actions (CNP+ full admin).

Hidden tabs simply do not apply to your role.

---

### G1 — Personnel tab

**Search:** type in the search box to filter by username, rank, or division name.

**Add Personnel** (if you have rights):

1. Click **+ Add Personnel**.
2. **Username:** Roblox or portal username (as your unit uses).
3. **Main Rank:** choose from the list (you cannot assign someone at or above your own level).
4. **Division:** choose their primary division.
5. **Divisional Rank** (optional): if your division has custom ranks in the system, you can pick one; permissions still follow the **mapped main rank**.
6. **Personnel office** checkboxes (OCNP/OCNO): only for admins who may assign staff affiliations.
7. **Account Active:** leave checked for a normal new account.
8. **Save.**

New accounts usually get password **`Username1234`** and must change password on first login.

**Edit:** click **Edit** on a row you are allowed to change; username is fixed after creation.

**OCNP/OCNO-only mode:** you may only work with **Navy Divisionless** accounts; division is fixed to NDVL.

**Account Active:** if unchecked, the user cannot sign in (this is stored in the portal database).

**Remove account permanently:** admins may also have a button to permanently remove an account (removes the Firebase Auth account and the Firestore user profile). This is irreversible and typically requires the command’s Cloud Functions to be deployed.

---

### G2 — Log Review tab

You see pending logs (your division, or **all divisions** if you are HQ).

For each row:

- Check **personnel**, **type**, **details**, **division**, and **proof** links.
- **Approve:** accepts the log; may notify Discord “approved” channel; may sync **quota** attendance; proof images may be removed from storage after approval (link may be marked as on Discord).
- **Reject:** optional reason; notifies Discord “pending” channel with rejection info.

After action, that row typically disappears from the list.

---

### G3 — My division (events) tab

For **MCPO+** who are not admiral-tier HQ:

- Edit the list of **event types** names your division can use on the Activity Log.
- Add types with the text box and **+ Add**, remove with **✕**, then **Save Event Types**.
- If the list is **empty**, the portal uses **global default** event types.

---

### G4 — Divisions tab

**Who sees it:** SecNav+ / CNP+ / Admiral+ in own division — exact **Edit** ability varies.

**Seed Default Divisions:** (SecNav+ only) one-click to create standard division documents if the database is empty.

**Add Division:** create a new division document.

**Edit** a division:

- **Name**, **short code**
- **Discord webhooks:** separate URLs for **pending** vs **approved** notifications; optional **legacy** single webhook fills gaps
- **Division ranks** (if your rank allows): custom titles mapped to main ranks for display
- **Event types** (if allowed): same idea as “My division” but in the modal

**Save** writes to Firestore.

---

### G5 — Archive tab

Archiving **does not delete history from statistics** in the way the Dashboard describes — archived copies are stored in **`archives`**, and monthly/all-time stats **still count** them. **Weekly** stats use **non-archived** approved logs only.

- **Archive My Division** (Admiral+): moves **all approved** logs for **your** division into an archive document and **removes** them from the live `logs` collection. Pending logs are untouched.
- **Archive Any Division** (Under SecNav+): choose a division or **all**; same effect at larger scope.

**This is described as irreversible** in the UI — treat it as a serious cleanup operation.

---

### G6 — Audit Log tab

Shows the last **100** audit entries: who did what, to which target, with JSON details. **Refresh** reloads.

---

## Part H — QUOTA Management (command)

You need **RDML+** (permission for division quota) **and** at least one division you are allowed to manage. Otherwise you see **access denied**.

### Division selector

Pick which division’s quota tools you are editing.

### Pending requests — all divisions (some HQ roles)

**CNP through Under Secretary** may see pending **MDQRA** and **LOA** requests for **non-Headquarters** divisions. They may be **view only** or able to approve/reject depending on the row’s division and your authority.

**Headquarters** quota requests are restricted to **Secretary of the Navy tier** and above in the rules.

### Tab: Requests

For the selected division, approve or reject **MDQRA** (percentage reduction) or **LOA** (leave window) requests. Reject may ask for an optional note.

### Tab: Event definitions

Map a **key** and **label** to either:

- a **standard event type**, or  
- an **exact custom event name**

so quota rules can match hosted events reliably. **Save definition** adds it; **Delete** removes.

### Tab: Quota policy

- Choose **rank** (main rank id), **period** (weekly or monthly), **effective from** (and optional **to**).
- **Rules:** use the **rule builder** (no JSON). Add one or more rules such as:
  - **Group (pick from pool):** “Complete 2 from: Training Exercise, Patrol”
  - **Mandatory (must-do):** “Inspection ×3”
  - **Duty minutes:** “120 duty minutes”
- **Save policy** stores it. The table below lists existing policies for the division.

### Tab: Reform list

- **Refresh:** reload the latest snapshot from the database.
- **Compute for this division:** recalculates who is behind quota for the **current** division and stores a snapshot (permission via Firestore rules).
- **Run assessment now** (SecNav+ / administrator tier): runs reform computation across divisions you manage (large operation — confirm prompt).

## Part I — Quota requests from the Dashboard (members)

If you see **Quota status** on the Dashboard:

### MDQRA

1. Enter a **percentage** from 1–100.
2. Enter a **reason** (justification).
3. Click **Submit MDQRA**.
4. Wait for success or error message. Your chain of command approves in **QUOTA Management**.

### LOA (leave of absence)

1. **LOA start** and **LOA end** — use date pickers.
2. **Reason.**
3. Click **Submit LOA.**

When approved, future quota calculations may treat you as **exempt** for overlapping periods (per system rules).

## Part J — Sign out and security habits

1. Click **Sign Out** in the header when you are done, especially on a **shared** computer.
2. Do not share your password.
3. If you suspect someone knows your password, ask an admin about reset procedures and change password as soon as you can.

## Part K — Troubleshooting

| Problem | What to try |
|--------|-------------|
| Blank page or errors | Refresh; try another browser; check internet. |
| “Permission denied” in Firestore | Your rank cannot perform that action — expected. |
| Cannot see Admin Panel | You are not in the allowed rank/staff list. |
| Event tab missing | Your division may be NDVL or unset; or only duty applies. |
| Attendee usernames fail | Spell usernames exactly; they must be in the **same division** as the log. |
| Image upload fails | File under 5 MB; use JPG/PNG/GIF/WebP. |
| Discord proof rejected | Paste the **full** message URL. |
| Quota shows “no policy” | Command has not published a policy for your **rank** in your **division** yet. |
| After approval, image link gone | Normal: admins may remove stored images after approval and rely on Discord. |
